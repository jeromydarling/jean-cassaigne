# The Resemblance — Music Reference Guide

Public domain Vietnamese folk music, K'Ho highland music, and Catholic chants for use in the play.
All folk melodies and plainchant are public domain. Live performance requires no licensing.
Specific studio/field recordings may require low-cost educational licensing — but for live performance by the chorus, the underlying melodies are fully free.

---

## Traditional Vietnamese Folk Songs

### "Bèo Dạt Mây Trôi" (Duckweed Drifting, Clouds Floating)
**Recommended scene:** Prologue (Jean digging the grave) + Final Scene (The Cabin) — Ka Trut's theme. Establish it here; its return in the final scene signals recognition.
- [Intimate guitar + voice version](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m691pStUcos) — Thu Le & Jim Kimo West
- [Full orchestral arrangement](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QNwcH6_TR4U) — ROCO, arr. Ngo Hoang Quan
- [Suntory Hall performance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l4SHNqIgLfk)

Northern folk ballad. Describes longing for a missing loved one. Haunting and slow. **Strongest candidate for Ka Trut's recurring musical signature.**

---

### "Lý Kéo Chài" (Fisherman's Rowing Song)
**Recommended scene:** Scene 2 (Caimon) — Jean's first posting in the Mekong Delta.
- [VTV World performance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sWj4yaIJEJc) — Sasha Mai & Kevin Rogers
- [Medley with Lý Cây Bông](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pi2Veh855L4)

Southern folk song from the Mekong Delta region. Direct geographic connection to the Caimon scene.

---

### "Lý Con Sáo" (The Starling Song)
**Recommended scene:** Scene 3 (The Blank Map) or Scene 8 (The Years) — background underscore, melancholic.
- [Full orchestral folk version](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6LWa77ly-JE)

One of the most beloved Vietnamese folk songs. Gentle, melancholic. Folk melody centuries old.

---

### "Lý Ngựa Ô" (The Black Stallion Song)
**Recommended scene:** Scene 4 (Village of Joy) or Scene 7 (The Return) — festive, processional energy.
- [Traditional recording](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UVsbpwPDVPo) — 19th century folk song
- [Jazz fusion version](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q6jZihNF9Aw) — Nguyên Lê (electric guitar), Cường Vũ (trumpet), Vân-Ánh Vanessa Võ (đàn tranh) — shows the song's range

Describes a groom decorating his black stallion for his wedding day. Celebratory, rhythmically driving.

---

### "Trống Cơm" (Rice Drum Song)
**Recommended scene:** Scene 4 (Village of Joy) — full chorus, ceremonial.
- [Civ 6 Vietnam theme](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pH5D30phvYs) — uses authentic t'rưng, đàn tranh, sáo trúc alongside this melody. Useful reference for how the instruments sound together.

Ancient ceremonial song. Chorus could perform live. The Civ 6 arrangement is a good orchestral reference for the instruments.

## K'Ho / Central Highlands Instruments

### T'rưng (Bamboo Xylophone)
**Recommended scenes:** Prologue, Scene 10 (The Needle), Final Scene (The Cabin) — Ka Trut's musical voice. Every time she appears, a single t'rưng phrase.
- [Solo instrument demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T-2EtojEDP0)
- [Performance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0scw1KZJIbY)

Traditional bamboo xylophone of the Central Highlands ethnic groups (Jarai, Bahnar, K'Ho). **Strongest recommendation as Ka Trut's recurring musical signature throughout the play.**

---

### K'Ho Music — Live Performances
**Recommended scene:** Scene 4 (Village of Joy) — full K'Ho ceremonial context.
- [Dalat night market K'Ho performance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hRmLyUVRgIY) — actual K'Ho people of Lâm Đồng province (Di Linh's region). Gongs, drums, gourd pipes.
- [K'Ho dance performance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fw-7jm7omH0) — full ceremonial context, traditional instruments listed

K'Ho are the oldest ethnic group in the Southern Central Highlands. Di Linh is in Lâm Đồng province. This is the music Jean heard daily.

---

### Central Highlands Gong Ensemble
**Recommended scene:** Scene 4 (Village of Joy), offstage in Scene 3 (The Blank Map).
- [UNESCO-recognized gong tradition, folk dance](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yFtVSOY2j6g)

Gong cultural space spans Kon Tum, Gia Lai, Dak Lak, Dak Nong, Lâm Đồng — recognized by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage. Ethnically includes the Coho (K'Ho) people. This is what Jean heard outside his window.

---

### Đàn Đá (Lithophone / Stone Xylophone)
**Recommended scenes:** Scene 3 (The Blank Map), ghost appearances of Ka Trut — eerie, otherworldly.
- [Ancient lithophone played in Tay Nguyen](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=trTDTCixA_c)
- [Craftsman preserving the tradition](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z5yngEiLxe4)

Struck stone slabs producing pitched tones. Found in the Central Highlands. Genuinely eerie sound. **Best option for Ka Trut's ghostly entrances.**

## Catholic Liturgical Chants

### "Veni Creator Spiritus"
**Recommended scene:** Scene 5 (The Consecration) — Jean ordained bishop on June 24, 1941. Also: Ka Trut's aside in Scene 2 about Jean's diaconate on Pentecost Ember Saturday.
- [Simple Gregorian chant](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5GrQJGQWfd8)
- [With English translation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q7EaOlCMPWU) — Adrian Fortescue translation
- [Parish choir version](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DcjtO4lJX1g) — closest to what your cantors would perform

Hymn to the Holy Spirit. Sung at ordinations, consecrations, and Pentecost. Jean was ordained deacon on Pentecost Ember Saturday (June 6, 1925). **The feast of tongues. He didn't know yet that was the assignment.**

### "Kyrie Eleison"
**Recommended scene:** Intermission (chorus in the aisles), candle procession after Scene 10 (The Needle).
- [Benedictine monks — 1 hour](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i5nTe2D-buo)

## Notes on Licensing

- **Completely free (live performance):** Any folk melody pre-1900, all plainchant. No licensing needed.
- **Requires low-cost license:** Specific studio/field recordings (Smithsonian Folkways, etc.) — not needed if performing live.
- **Best resource to contact:** Vietnamese Institute for Musicology (Viện Âm Nhạc), Hanoi — active cultural preservation, may assist for free given the play's subject.
- **Twin Cities resource:** MacPhail Center for Music and Ordway Center have worked with Vietnamese community musicians. Your Vietnamese Catholic parishes may have musicians who know these traditions firsthand.
